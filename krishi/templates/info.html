Major Crops Grown in India Category: Indian Geography On April 17, 2014 By Suraj Yadav The major crops that are grown in India are mentioned below in this article. Food crops: Rice and Wheat are cereals. They are starchy seeds. They are nutritive. They form basic diet of mankind. Plantation crops: Tea and Coffee are called plantation crops. Both are used as drinks of mild stimulants. They are beverages. Each of them is a plantation crop. In plantation agriculture, some aspects of industrial processing are found. It differs from common agriculture widely. In fact it is more an industry than agriculture or it may be called both agriculture and industry. It is an agriculture which is carried on through modern industrial management and organization. Large scale capital investment, large scale estates (Garden), factories on the estates, employment of large number of employees of all categories, – field workers, office staff, managerial personnel, use of mechanical equipment, organized marketing facilities, etc. are the characteristics of a plantation agriculture. Fiber Crops: Cotton and Jute are called fiber crops. Both are vegetable fibers.

Rice

Rice is grown under varying conditions in India from 8° to 25° N latitude and from sea level to about 2,500 metre altitude. It is a tropical plant and requires high heat and high humidity for its successful growth. The temperature should be fairly high at mean monthly of 24°C. It should be 20°- 22°C at the time of sowing, 23°-25°C during growth and 25°-30°C at the harvesting time. The average annual rainfall required by rice is 150 cm.
